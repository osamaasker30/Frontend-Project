Aleppo Citadel A fortified palace dating back to the Middle Ages. The Citadel of Aleppo is considered one of the oldest and largest castles in the world. The hill on which the citadel is located dates back to the third millennium BC, when it was later occupied by many civilizations, including the Greeks, Byzantines, Mamluks and Ayyubids, while it appears that most of the current construction dates back to the Ayyubid period. The Aga Khan Trust for Culture and the Archaeological Society of Aleppo carried out extensive conservation operations on it in 2000.

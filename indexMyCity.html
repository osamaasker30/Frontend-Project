The Syrian city
of Aleppo

The city of Aleppo is an Arab city affiliated to the Arab Republic of Syria. It is located in the northern part of Syria at a latitude of 36.2053 degrees north of the equator, and a longitude of 37.1590 degrees east of Greenwich. Its land area is 190 kmÂ², and it is one of the largest cities in the regions of the Levant. Administratively, it belongs to the Aleppo Governorate, which arose in the year 10,000 BC , and passed through it through the ages of many nations and civilizations. such as Aramaic, Assyrian, Hellenic, Roman, Persian, Byzantine, and Islamic.

Information about
the Syrian city
of Aleppo

It is called Al-Shahba, and it rises 1,240 feet above sea level.
Its population is 5,132,10 people, according to the 2014 statistics, and its population consists of many ethnic groups, such as Arabs who are the majority, and minorities of Turkmen, Circassians, Kurds, and Armenians.
The Arabic language is the official language in it, and it is characterized by being mixed with some Turkish words.

economy in the
city of Aleppo

The city of Aleppo is considered one of the largest and richest Syrian cities, and it is the most important industrial center in Syria. However, its classification varies according to the references, not even within the same reference. While the French version of Wikipedia classifies it as the second largest Syrian city, it was mentioned in the Arabic version that it is the largest city in Syria, and it is the capital of Aleppo Governorate, which is the largest Syrian governorate in terms of population.

Aleppo now

Aleppo after
the war

The Syrian city of Aleppo was the main focus of the Syrian conflict between the opposition fighters on the one hand, and the Syrian army supported by Russia and Iran on the other.
Many archaeologists agree that Old Aleppo is a museum that includes the most amazing buildings.
The mosques, temples, caravanserais, and adjacent and symmetrical markets are among the most prominent landmarks in the city.
The conflict in Syria caused destruction and looting of many of the heritage and archaeological treasures in Syria, which were described as an "open-air museum."

The amount of
destruction
in Aleppo

The United Nations Educational, Scientific and Cultural Organization (UNESCO) mentioned in a report the extent of the damage in the archaeological sites of Aleppo, indicating that a third of the city was completely destroyed during the war years, and 60 percent of the old city was badly damaged, while 30 percent was completely destroyed.

education
in Aleppo

The city of Aleppo has been affected in an unimaginable way in the field of education after it had the best university in the Arab countries. Schools were destroyed and thousands of students were deprived of completing their studies. The situation is still the same as some children are trying to convince themselves that they are in a dream in order to escape from this painful reality
